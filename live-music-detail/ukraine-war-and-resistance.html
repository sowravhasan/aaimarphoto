Ukraine: War and Resistance

Ukraine | February 2022 - Present

Russia's invasion of Ukraine on February 24, 2022, marked the beginning of the largest conventional war in Europe since World War II. What Vladimir Putin described as a "special military operation" quickly evolved into a full-scale conflict that has reshaped global geopolitics and brought devastating consequences to millions of Ukrainians.

The Ukrainian resistance, led by President Volodymyr Zelensky, defied initial expectations of a swift Russian victory. From the successful defense of Kyiv in the early days of the war to the liberation of territories in Kharkiv and Kherson, Ukrainian forces have demonstrated remarkable resilience and tactical innovation in the face of a numerically superior enemy.

Civilian populations have borne the brunt of the conflict, with Russian forces targeting critical infrastructure including power plants, hospitals, and schools. The siege of Mariupol, the bombing of theaters and maternity wards, and mass deportations have drawn widespread international condemnation and war crimes investigations.

Ukraine's digital resistance has been as significant as its military response. The country has leveraged technology and social media to maintain international support, document atrocities, and coordinate defense efforts. President Zelensky's video addresses to world leaders and parliaments have kept Ukraine's struggle in the global spotlight.

The humanitarian impact has been staggering, with millions of Ukrainians displaced internally or seeking refuge in neighboring countries. Poland, Romania, and other European nations have opened their borders to accommodate the largest refugee crisis in Europe since World War II, fundamentally altering the continent's demographic and political landscape.

International solidarity with Ukraine has manifested in unprecedented military aid, economic sanctions against Russia, and efforts to isolate Moscow diplomatically. However, the conflict has also exposed global divisions, particularly among developing nations reluctant to choose sides in what many view as a geopolitical struggle between great powers.

As the war continues, Ukraine's resistance has become a symbol of democratic resilience against authoritarian aggression. The conflict has redefined European security architecture, strengthened NATO, and forced a fundamental reassessment of the post-Cold War order that has shaped international relations for over three decades.
