Haiti: Chaos and Violence

Port-au-Prince, Haiti | 2021-2024

Haiti has descended into unprecedented chaos and violence since the assassination of President Jovenel Moïse in July 2021. What was already the poorest country in the Western Hemisphere has been further destabilized by gang warfare, political vacuum, and humanitarian crisis that has left millions struggling for survival.

The capital, Port-au-Prince, has become a battleground controlled largely by armed gangs. The G9 alliance, led by former police officer Jimmy "Barbecue" Chérizier, controls an estimated 60% of the metropolitan area. These gangs have taken control of critical infrastructure, including the main port and fuel terminals, effectively holding the entire country hostage.

Daily life in Haiti has become a constant struggle for survival. Kidnappings occur regularly, with gangs targeting both wealthy individuals and ordinary citizens for ransom. Schools, hospitals, and businesses have been forced to close as violence erupts unpredictably across neighborhoods. Many residents are trapped in their homes, afraid to venture out for basic necessities.

The humanitarian situation is catastrophic. According to the UN, over 4.7 million people - nearly half the population - need humanitarian assistance. Acute malnutrition affects hundreds of thousands of children, while cholera has returned after years of absence. Access to clean water, healthcare, and education has been severely compromised.

The Haitian National Police (HNP), undermanned and outgunned, struggles to maintain order against well-armed gangs. Many officers have been killed, kidnapped, or have fled the country. International calls for intervention have been met with mixed responses, as Haitians themselves remain divided on foreign assistance given the country's troubled history with international interventions.

Economic collapse has compounded the crisis. The Haitian gourde has lost significant value, inflation has soared, and unemployment is widespread. The gangs' control of fuel supplies has paralyzed transportation and caused severe shortages of basic goods, creating a vicious cycle of scarcity and violence.

International efforts to address the crisis have included various diplomatic initiatives and humanitarian aid, but the situation continues to deteriorate. The deployment of a Kenya-led international force has been announced, though its effectiveness remains to be seen in addressing the root causes of Haiti's multifaceted crisis.
