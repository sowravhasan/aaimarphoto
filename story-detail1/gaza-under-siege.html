Gaza: Under Siege

Gaza Strip | October 2023 - Present

The Gaza Strip, a narrow coastal enclave home to over 2.3 million Palestinians, has been under an Israeli and Egyptian blockade since 2007. This densely populated territory, measuring just 365 square kilometers, has become synonymous with one of the world's most intractable conflicts and ongoing humanitarian crises.

The current escalation began on October 7, 2023, when Hamas launched a surprise attack on Israel, killing over 1,200 people and taking more than 240 hostages. Israel's response has been swift and devastating, with a military campaign aimed at destroying Hamas's military capabilities and securing the release of hostages. The conflict has resulted in tens of thousands of Palestinian casualties and the displacement of the majority of Gaza's population.

Life in Gaza has become a daily struggle for survival. The blockade has severely restricted the movement of people and goods, creating chronic shortages of essential supplies including food, medicine, fuel, and construction materials. Unemployment rates exceed 40%, and the majority of the population relies on international aid for basic necessities.

The territory's infrastructure has been systematically degraded by years of conflict and blockade. The electricity grid provides power for only a few hours per day, water treatment facilities operate at reduced capacity, and the healthcare system struggles to meet basic needs. Schools and hospitals have been damaged or destroyed in successive military operations, further undermining the population's access to essential services.

Children bear a disproportionate burden of the crisis. UNICEF estimates that children make up nearly half of Gaza's population, and many have experienced multiple traumatic events including military operations, displacement, and the loss of family members. Educational opportunities are limited, with many schools operating in shifts due to overcrowding and damage to facilities.

The international community has repeatedly called for a resolution to the crisis, with numerous UN resolutions condemning the blockade and calling for its lifting. However, political divisions and security concerns have prevented meaningful progress. Humanitarian organizations continue to provide assistance, but their efforts are hampered by access restrictions and recurring violence.

As the current conflict continues, the humanitarian situation in Gaza remains dire. The territory's isolation, combined with the ongoing violence, has created a crisis that affects every aspect of daily life for Palestinians trapped within its borders. The international community continues to grapple with finding a sustainable solution that addresses both security concerns and the fundamental human rights of Gaza's population.
