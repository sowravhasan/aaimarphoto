The War in Lebanon

Lebanon | October 2024

The recent war in Lebanon between Israel and Hezbollah is closely linked to escalating regional tensions, particularly the ongoing conflict in Gaza. What began as minor hostilities after October 7, 2023, spiraled into full-scale conflict when Israel launched Operation Northern Arrows in September 2024, targeting Hezbollah's leadership and infrastructure.

This escalation has devastated Lebanon's already fragile infrastructure and displaced hundreds of thousands of civilians. The southern regions, including Nabatieh and Tyre, have borne the brunt of the violence, with entire neighborhoods reduced to rubble. Hospitals struggle to cope with the influx of casualties while running low on medical supplies due to blockades and damaged transport routes.

The civilian population finds itself caught between two powerful military forces. In the south, families have been forced to abandon their homes, often with little more than the clothes on their backs. The displaced seek shelter in schools, mosques, and with relatives in safer areas, but resources are stretched thin across the country.

International humanitarian organizations face significant challenges in delivering aid due to active combat zones and damaged infrastructure. The Lebanese government, already dealing with a severe economic crisis since 2019, lacks the resources to adequately respond to the humanitarian emergency. The situation is particularly dire for Palestinian refugees in Lebanon, who face additional restrictions on movement and access to services.

The war has also reignited sectarian tensions within Lebanon, as different communities react differently to Hezbollah's involvement in the conflict. While some support the group's resistance against Israel, others blame it for bringing destruction to the country. This internal division complicates efforts to find a unified response to the crisis and raises concerns about Lebanon's long-term stability.

As diplomatic efforts continue to seek a ceasefire, the human cost continues to mount. The war has highlighted Lebanon's vulnerability as a state caught between regional powers and the devastating impact of proxy conflicts on civilian populations.
