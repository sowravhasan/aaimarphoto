Haiti: Chaos and Violence

Port-au-Prince, Haiti | July 2021 - Present

Haiti plunged into unprecedented chaos following the assassination of President Jovenel Moïse on July 7, 2021. The killing of the president at his private residence marked the beginning of a complete breakdown of state authority and the emergence of powerful gangs that now control an estimated 60% of the country, including most of the capital, Port-au-Prince.

Gang violence has reached levels that have shocked even Haiti, a country long accustomed to political instability and violence. The G9 alliance, led by former police officer Jimmy "Barbecue" Chérizier, has emerged as the most powerful force in the country, controlling ports, fuel terminals, and key infrastructure while demanding the resignation of Prime Minister Ariel Henry.

The humanitarian crisis has reached catastrophic proportions, with over 4.7 million Haitians facing acute hunger and more than 200,000 people displaced from their homes. Hospitals have been forced to close due to fuel shortages and gang attacks, while schools remain shuttered, leaving an entire generation of children without education.

Kidnappings have become a daily occurrence, with gangs targeting everyone from foreign missionaries to local businesspeople and ordinary citizens. The Haitian National Police, understaffed and outgunned, struggle to maintain any semblance of order as officers flee the country or join the very gangs they once fought against.

International efforts to restore stability have been complicated by Haiti's rejection of foreign military intervention and the failure of previous international missions. The UN and regional powers have struggled to find effective ways to support the Haitian people while respecting the country's sovereignty and avoiding the mistakes of past interventions.

The economic collapse has been total, with the Haitian gourde losing more than 50% of its value in 2022 alone. Basic services like water and electricity are virtually non-existent in many areas, while the cholera outbreak that began in October 2022 has added another layer to the crisis facing ordinary Haitians.

Haiti's descent into chaos represents one of the most complete state failures in the Western Hemisphere. As gangs continue to expand their control and humanitarian conditions deteriorate, the international community faces the challenge of helping a proud nation that has repeatedly rejected external solutions to its internal problems.
