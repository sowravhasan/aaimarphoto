Ukraine: War and Resistance

Ukraine | February 2022 - Present

The full-scale Russian invasion of Ukraine on February 24, 2022, marked a turning point in European security and sparked the largest military conflict on the continent since World War II. What began as a "special military operation" in Russia's words has evolved into a prolonged war that has reshaped global geopolitics and tested international resolve.

The initial phase of the invasion saw rapid advances by Russian forces toward multiple Ukrainian cities, including the capital Kyiv. However, unexpectedly fierce Ukrainian resistance, bolstered by Western military aid and intelligence support, stalled the Russian advance. The failed assault on Kyiv and the subsequent Russian withdrawal from northern Ukraine marked the first major setback for Russian forces.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy emerged as a global symbol of resistance, refusing evacuation offers and remaining in Kyiv to lead his country's defense. His daily video addresses to the nation and appeals to international partners have galvanized both domestic morale and international support for Ukraine's cause.

The human cost has been enormous. Thousands of civilians have been killed, millions have been displaced, and entire cities like Mariupol, Bakhmut, and Avdiivka have been reduced to rubble. The siege of Mariupol and the defense of the Azovstal steel plant became symbols of Ukrainian resistance, while discoveries of civilian casualties in places like Bucha shocked the international community.

The conflict has evolved through several phases, from the initial invasion attempt to the focus on eastern and southern Ukraine, to Ukraine's successful counteroffensives in Kharkiv and Kherson regions. The introduction of advanced Western weapons systems, including HIMARS, ATACMS, and main battle tanks, has gradually shifted the balance, though the front lines have largely stabilized into a war of attrition.

Beyond the battlefield, the war has had global implications. Energy supplies, food security, and international law have all been affected. Russia's weaponization of energy exports, attacks on Ukrainian grain infrastructure, and nuclear threats have created worldwide concerns about escalation and global stability.

As the conflict enters its third year, both sides face challenges of sustaining their war efforts. Ukraine continues to seek additional military aid and EU/NATO membership, while Russia grapples with international sanctions, domestic pressures, and the sustainability of its military campaign. The outcome will likely determine the future security architecture of Europe and the principle of territorial integrity worldwide.
