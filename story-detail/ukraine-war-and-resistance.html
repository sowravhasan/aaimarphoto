Ukraine: War and Resistance

Ukraine | 2022-2024

The Russian invasion of Ukraine on February 24, 2022, marked the beginning of the largest military conflict in Europe since World War II. What was expected by many to be a swift conquest turned into a prolonged conflict that has reshaped geopolitical alliances and tested the resolve of both nations.

The initial assault targeted multiple fronts, with Russian forces advancing toward Kyiv, Ukraine's capital, while launching attacks on major cities including Kharkiv, Mariupol, and Kherson. The siege of Mariupol became a symbol of Ukrainian resistance, with defenders holding out for months in the Azovstal steel plant despite overwhelming odds.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy emerged as an unlikely wartime leader, rallying international support through social media and direct appeals to world leaders. His refusal to evacuate Kyiv and his famous response "I need ammunition, not a ride" became defining moments of the conflict.

The war has displaced millions of Ukrainians, creating the largest refugee crisis in Europe since World War II. The international community responded with unprecedented sanctions against Russia and military aid to Ukraine, fundamentally altering the global security landscape.

Civilian infrastructure has been deliberately targeted in what experts consider war crimes, with attacks on power grids, hospitals, and schools. Yet Ukrainian resistance has been remarkable, with successful counteroffensives retaking significant territory, including the liberation of Kharkiv region and Kherson city.
